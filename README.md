## Project Objective
The goal of this project is to build a robust **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)** system capable of answering complex queries about South Asian cuisine. To ensure maximum efficiency and accuracy, we implemented a **Rigorous Ablation Study**, evaluating different combinations of Chunking, Retrieval, and Prompting strategies.

---

## System Architecture & Codebase Overview
All core logic is consolidated into a single Jupyter Notebook (`RAG_Pipeline_Codebase.ipynb`). The pipeline is divided into the following sequential modules:

1. **SETUP & Data Ingestion (Cell 1 & 2):** Scrapes raw HTML content from Wikipedia, Wikibooks, and specialized culinary blogs. Cleans and extracts text/tables while preserving metadata.
2. **Chunking Strategies (Cell 3):** * *Baseline:* 800-character flat chunking.
   * *Advanced:* Hierarchical Parent-Child chunking (small children for search, large parents for context).
3. **Decoupled Vectorization & Prompting (Cell 4 & 5):** * *Baseline:* `all-MiniLM-L6-v2` with FAISS Flat index.
   * *Advanced:* `bge-small-en-v1.5` with FAISS HNSW index + MS-MARCO Cross-Encoder Reranking.
4. **Inference Engines (Cell 6):** Object-oriented classes (`BaselineRAG` and `AdvancedRAG`) utilizing the **Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct** model running in FP16 precision.
5. **Comprehensive Evaluation (Cell 7 & 8):** Runs 6 decoupled pipelines (P1-P6). Scoring relies on **Mean-Pooling Semantic Match** and **Lexical F1 Overlap**.

## Ablation Study & Key Findings

We evaluated 6 different pipeline configurations to isolate the impact of our structural upgrades. The chart (`decoupled_comparison_chart.png`) highlights a highly valuable and counter-intuitive finding.

### The Winning Pipeline: **P4 (Advanced Prompt Only)**
* **Configuration:** Baseline Chunking + Baseline Retrieval + Advanced Prompt.
* **Performance:** Achieved the highest scores overall (Semantic: **73.6**, Composite: **61.7**).

### Scientific Conclusion & Insights
Contrary to the initial assumption that "more complex is better", our ablation study proved that:
1. **Prompting is Paramount:** The small-scale Qwen-0.5B model benefited massively from the "Advanced Prompt" (strict data extraction instructions), which prevented hallucination and conversational filler.
2. **Context Overload:** Introducing **Parent-Child Chunking (P2)** and **Advanced Retrieval (P3)** actually *degraded* performance compared to the Baseline (P1). 
   * *Why?* For a 0.5B parameter model, providing longer "parent" chunks or highly complex cross-encoder reranked contexts likely introduced noise. The small attention window of the LLM became overwhelmed, leading to lower extraction accuracy. 
3. **Ultimate RAG (P6):** Even when combining all advanced features, it still slightly underperformed P4, further proving that keeping the retrieval simple (Flat 800-char chunks) paired with strict prompt engineering is the optimal strategy for this specific hardware/model constraint.

## Directory Guide
To keep the project clean, the notebook automatically generates and routes files into the following directories:
* `data/raw/`: Raw scraped JSON corpus.
* `data/chunked/`: Processed fragments separated by strategy.
* `data/indices/`: Pre-computed FAISS vector databases (`.bin`).
* `results/payloads/`: JSON outputs for all 6 experiment pipelines, plus the final `output_payload.json` generated by P4.
* `results/plots/`: Contains the `decoupled_comparison_chart.png` for the final presentation.
